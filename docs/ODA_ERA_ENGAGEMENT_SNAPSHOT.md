## What the data says

1. **Tic Tac Toe is the #1 most-played game** (385 plays) — and it's already #2 in the AMG
   Studios app queue. The kids independently validated the roadmap.
2. **Connect 4 has the WIDEST reach** — 34 of 47 kids (72%) played it. Drop4-as-the-premium-
   Connect-4 swap is aimed at the single most universal game on the platform.
3. **Floppy Bird is a retention monster** (376 plays from just 17 kids = 22 plays/kid — the
   highest depth on the platform, driven by the slow easy mode younger kids loved). "Chill
   Mode" deserves to be a first-class feature.
4. **3PT Showdown earned real numbers** (24 players, 141 plays) despite being rough — a 3D
   game held its own on Chromebooks. The rebuild-with-your-character plan has proven demand.
5. Head-to-head games dominate the top 10 (TTT, C4, hangman, uno, RPS, ping pong) — kids came
   to play EACH OTHER. Multiplayer/tournament infrastructure is the moat; keep investing there.
6. Helicopter's low numbers (4 players) match Devon's instinct that it needs work — but low
   reach also means low risk to overhaul it completely.
7. Chess barely registered (3 players) with this age group — fine to keep, don't prioritize.

Hero-game shortlist confirmed by data: **Tic Tac Toe, Connect 4 (Drop4 swap), Floppy Bird,
3PT Showdown, Typing Race** — with Hangman/Uno/RPS as the second wave.
